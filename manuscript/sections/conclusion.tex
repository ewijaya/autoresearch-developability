\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We demonstrate that the autoresearch paradigm extends from neural architecture search to multi-objective peptide ranking policy search.
On a public benchmark of 3{,}554 antimicrobial peptides, 100 autonomous experiments produce a ranking policy that outperforms NSGA-II (0.650 vs.\ 0.444 top-$k$ enrichment) and best-of-1{,}000 random weight search (0.650 vs.\ 0.611); the margin over equal-weight scalarization is cross-split-consistent ($0.650 \pm 0.034$ vs.\ $0.585 \pm 0.050$, Wilcoxon $p = 0.004$) though single-split bootstrap CIs overlap.
The agent discovered structural innovations (oracle consensus voting, reciprocal rank fusion, learned reranking) that go beyond the policy class accessible to random search.
This work is a proof-of-concept on public data with acknowledged limitations.
We release the full pipeline as open-source software, designed for portability to proprietary data; natural next steps are sequence-similarity-aware splitting, non-AMP peptide classes, and integration with live assay feedback.
